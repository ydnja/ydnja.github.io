Confronting the Issues of an Aging Society
An issue every country will face? An Aging society

The median age of many European countries are well over 40: Germany (45.7), Italy (47.3), Spain (44.9).
An aging society means more retirees and less working population (Dependency ratio grows).
For countries with less robust social welfare programmes, the issue is less pronounced:
For countries that fund social welfare programmes with high tax rates (ie. Germany, Finland, Italy, Spain etc.),
increasing tax rates may not be an option but neither is cutting social welfare programmes.
